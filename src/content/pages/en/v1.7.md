Super Mario Worker Project has received its largest update ever - v1.7 (Rebirth Update), now updated to `v1.7.12`.

SMWP v1.7 added a variety of facilities, including Message Block, Switch Blocks, Ice Block, Special Solids, Fluid Controller, Scene Controller, View Controller, and Imitators; introduced the global lava and auto fluid system; feature improvements include Beetroot’s ability to break facility blocks, Fast Retry for shorter revival delay, and the introduction of the Pick key for Picking and Parameterized Object Movement.

## Blocks, Imitators & Backgrounds
The Blocks selector has been reorganized: the original first page (classic MW 1.1 page) has been removed, the third page (formerly the fourth page) has been rearranged and now includes the Brown Castle and Fortress tiles, and a new fourth page has been added, featuring the Solid Color tiles introduced in v1.7.11.

Introducing Imitators. An Imitator is classified as a scenery object and shares the same layer as other scenery. It can imitate any Block, and is displayed in the editor as a semi-transparent version of that Block.

Pick key also works with Imitators. While holding an Imitator, simply press `E` on any Block or Imitator to change the held Imitator into that Block.

Starting with v1.7.7, you can press the middle mouse button to switch between Block and Imitator placement modes. A dedicated cursor is displayed while in Imitator mode. In v1.7.12, the cursor's transparency in Imitator mode was further optimized.

## Enemies
SMWP v1.7 introduced Shells, including the four colored Koopa shells and the Buzzy Beetle shell. Each shell can be placed in either a stationary or moving initial state, and its state can be controlled via the secondary function of the Green Switch Block.

v1.7.11 added the Blue Piranha Plant, Stone Piranha Plant, and Evil Lakitu.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-1711-buddies.webp" />
</div>

- **Blue Piranha Plant**: Cannot be killed by fireballs; emerges even if Mario approaches; is destroyed together with any type of moving shell upon collision.
- **Stone Piranha Plant**: Cannot be killed by fireballs; does not emerge when Mario approaches; can be killed by Buzzy Beetle shells, and can destroy colored shells.
- **Evil Lakitu**: Cannot be killed by fireballs; throws gray Spiny Eggs, which turn into Stone Spinies upon landing.

v1.7.12 added four-directional Piranha Heads and four-directional Spikes.

</div>

v1.7.12 added a new Thwomp Activate Blocks option to Additional Settings. When enabled, Thwomps can activate or break interactive blocks, including ?-Blocks, Bricks, Message Blocks, Switch Blocks, Fluid Controller Blocks and Ice Blocks.

## Fluid System
Additional Settings now includes two new fluid-related options: Fluid Type and Auto Fluid. Fluid Type determines whether the fluid is water or lava, while Auto Fluid allows the fluid level to rise and fall automatically in a loop, with configurable movement speed and an initial delay.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-fluid-additional.webp" />
</div>

Three types of Fluid Controllers have been added to Marks: Block (reusable), Block (single-use), and Area (invisible).

  <img src="/images/smwp-fluid-block1.webp" />
  <img src="/images/smwp-fluid-block2.webp" />

For all three types of Fluid Controllers, you can configure the target fluid level and the rising/falling speed.

  <img src="/images/smwp-fluid-speed.webp" />

Additionally, if a Cheep Cheep leaves the water while the fluid level is changing or the fluid type is being switched, it will immediately transform into the corresponding colored Flying Cheep Cheep.

## Weather System
v1.7.12 introduced a weather system with five selectable weather types: Rainy, Falling Stars, Snowy, Thunder, and Windy. Each weather type supports multiple intensity levels.
<div style="text-align:center;">

  <img src="/images/smwp-weather-demo.webp" />
</div>

Two new settings have also been added: Dark Level and Bright Level.  
Dark Level ranges from 0 to 9. Level 0 represents normal lighting, and higher levels make the stage progressively darker. At Level 9, the stage becomes completely dark.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-dark-demo.webp" />
</div>

Bright Level allows various objects to emit light in dark environments. It ranges from 0 to 5, where 0 disables illumination entirely, and higher levels increase the lighting radius.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-bright-demo.webp" />
</div>

Whether an object emits light is fully customizable. In the selector, objects that support illumination display a small light bulb icon in the upper-right corner. Right-click the icon to toggle illumination for that object - the bulb lights up when illumination is enabled.

  <img src="/images/smwp-bright-toggle.webp" />

Additional Settings also allows light emission to be enabled for objects generated by both the player and enemies. Supported objects include Mario's fireballs, beetroots, Starman Mario, Piranha Plant fireballs, Bullet Bills, hammers, Bowser's flame, and Fire Bro. fireballs.

## Marks
### Message Block
After placing a Message Block, you can enter a custom message. When activated, the message is displayed on screen. The message disappears when the block is activated again or when another Message Block is triggered.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-message.webp" />
</div>

### Ice Block
Walking on Ice Blocks is more slippery. When an Ice Block is hit by a fireball or a Thwomp with the Thwomp Activate Blocks option enabled, it develops cracks. A cracked Ice Block melts when hit again. Ice Blocks also melt instantly if submerged in lava fluid.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-ice.webp" />
</div>

### Special Solids
v1.7 introduced three new Special Solids (shown from left to right: Solid, Half-Solid, One-Way Solid, and Platform Solid).
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-solid.webp" />
</div>

A Half-Solid is half the height of a regular Solid, making it ideal for Platform tiles. A One-Way Solid allows Mario and other entities, such as Blue Paratroopas, to pass through from below. A Platform Solid can be thought of as a One-Way Solid that only Mario can stand on, effectively behaving like a stationary (not moving) platform.

### View Controller
A View Controller lets you define a red rectangular region that is visible only in the editor. If Mario spawns or exits a passage within this region, the camera will be confined to its boundaries and will not move beyond them. This object can be used to create bonus rooms.

### Scene Controller
A Scene Controller can control the music, background, and fluid state. Its settings are applied immediately when Mario comes into contact with the controller.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-scenectl-bgm.webp" />
  <img src="/images/smwp-scenectl-bgp.webp" />
  <img src="/images/smwp-scenectl-fluid.webp" />
</div>

A Scene Controller can also be linked to a View Controller or Bowser. Place it one tile to the right of a View Controller or Bowser, and its settings will be applied automatically when Mario activates the View Controller or triggers the Bowser battle.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-scenectl-collab.webp" />
</div>

### Switch Blocks
Switch Blocks come in three object types (Switch, Type A, and Type B) and eight colors. Type A has no collision, while Type B is solid. Whenever a Switch of a given color is triggered, all Type A and Type B blocks of the same color swap states.
<div style="text-align:center;">
  <img src="/images/smwp-switch.webp" />
</div>

SMWP introduces an Advanced Switch option in Additional Settings. When set to `YES`, each Switch Block gains a unique secondary function:
- **Red**: Switches the fluid between water and lava. Fluid Controllers also change color accordingly.
- **Yellow**: Swaps coins and bricks.
- **Green**: Makes all stationary shells start moving, and all moving shells stop. A shell that moves for the first time starts moving to the left by default; if it has moved before, it resumes its previous direction.
- **Cyan**: Reverses the direction of all moving platforms.
- **Blue**: Hides/restores the fluid. Fluid level remains changing while hidden, as well as the Red Switch's secondary effect, continues to function normally.
- **Purple**: Reverses the rotation direction of all Roto-Discs.
- **White**: Controls the primary function of all colored Type A/Type B blocks except black.
- **Black**: Controls the secondary functions of all colors except white.

v1.7.4 added the Celeste Style Switch option to Additional Settings. When disabled, if a Type A block beneath Mario switches to Type B, Mario becomes trapped inside it. When enabled, the transformation is deferred until Mario leaves the block, preventing him from getting stuck.

v1.7.7 added Accessibility Mode (Color). When enabled, colored Switch Blocks display letters in both the editor and gameplay to help distinguish their colors. This setting is controlled by the player rather than the level author. v1.7.7 also added a functionality to allow players to temporarily mute Switch Block sound effects for the current level..

Starting with v1.7.11, while placing Type A or Type B blocks in the editor, you can press the middle mouse button to switch between the two directly.

## Key Binding Adjustments
The `Ctrl` and `Shift` key functions in previous versions were reassigned to `Z` and `Q` in v1.7, respectively. A new `E` key was also introduced. In Block and Imitator modes, `E` acts as a Picker, allowing the selected Block or Imitator to copy the Block currently under the cursor. When editing certain Marks (such as Flying Cheep Cheep areas), holding `E` while hovering over the corresponding object and moving the mouse lets you reposition parameterized objects directly. Starting with v1.7.2, all three of these keys can be remapped.

The original `F5` function was removed in v1.7. From v1.7.2, `F5` was reassigned to toggle the visibility of Solids in the editor. This feature was further improved in v1.7.11: when hidden solids are enabled, selecting any solid object automatically turns solid visibility back on.

Starting with v1.7.12, the Restart Level key (default: `Q`) can also be remapped.

## Music Updates
### Expanded Built-in Music Library
v1.7.9 greatly expanded SMWP's built-in music library:

- **Super Mario Series (2)**: Added 20 selected tracks from official Super Mario games, including `SMG BEACH BOWL GALAXY`, which seamlessly switches between its above-water and underwater melodies.
- **Softendo Series**: Selected tracks from *New Super Mario Forever 2012*, *Mario Forever Galaxy*, and *Mario Forever Block Party*.
- **SMWP Original Series**: 13 original compositions created specifically for SMWP by **1168438795** and **Bluesun**.
- **Boss Music**: A collection of boss battle themes from *Mario Forever* and various official Mario games. These can be used not only for Bowser battles (by linking Bowser with a Scene Controller to switch the BGM), but also as regular level background music.

### Custom Music
v1.7.11 introduced Custom Music, allowing level creators to use their own music in levels.

To use this feature, level authors need to create a **music pack**.

#### Creating a Music Pack
1. Choose a name for your music pack. It is recommended to use a unique name to avoid conflicts with music packs created by other users.
2. Create a new folder using the music pack name.
3. Place all desired music files (OGG or MP3 is recommended) into this folder. The filenames can be the original song titles or any temporary names or IDs you prefer.
4. Create the music list. Inside the folder, create a file named `ListConfig.ini`, open it with a text editor, and list the filenames of all music files.
    <div style="text-align:center;">
      <img src="/images/smwp-listconfig.webp" />
    </div>
5. When editing `ListConfig.ini`, keep the following in mind:
    1. Enter one filename per line, with no blank lines between entries.
    2. Use the complete filename, including its extension. Each entry must exactly match the corresponding music file in the folder. If you rename a music file, remember to update `ListConfig.ini` as well.
    3. SMWP loads custom music according to the contents of `ListConfig.ini`. Therefore, both the filenames and their order determine which tracks are available and the order in which they appear in the Custom Music list.

#### Loading a Music Pack
1. Place the music pack folder into the `\Data\Custom\` directory inside your SMWP folder.
2. Launch SMWP and select `Options` from the main menu.
3. In the Options menu, click the entry labeled `Custom Music Package`, then enter the name of your music pack.
    <div style="text-align:center;">
      <img src="/images/smwp-custom-music-option.webp" />
    </div>

#### Testing and Using a Music Pack While Creating Levels
1. Load the desired music pack first.
2. In the editor, open the music selection screen and choose `Custom Music` from the bottom of the category list on the right.
    <div style="text-align:center;">
      <img src="/images/smwp-custom-music-editor.webp" />
    </div>
3. If the music pack has been loaded successfully, the Custom Music screen displays 10 tracks per page. Use `PREV` and `NEXT` to switch pages.
4. If you modify the music files or `ListConfig.ini` while the Custom Music screen is open, click `Refresh` to reload the music list.
5. Select a track from the Custom Music list as the level's background music.
6. When creating a scenario, **enter the name of the music pack folder**. When distributing your scenario, place the music pack folder in the same directory as the `.smws`/`.smwp` file so it serves as the dedicated music pack for your scenario.
    <div style="text-align:center;">
      <img src="/images/smwp-custom-music-smws.webp" />
    </div>

#### Using Music Packs When Playing Levels
When playing a scenario, SMWP first looks for the music pack's `ListConfig.ini` in the same directory as the scenario. If it is not found there, SMWP searches the `\Data\Custom\` directory instead.

When playing a level with custom music, place the music pack folder in SMWP's `\Data\Custom\` directory and load it from the Options menu before starting the level.

If you are working on multiple projects that use different music packs, remember to update the **Custom Music Package** setting in SMWP before editing or testing each project.

## Other Changes
- Beetroots can now interact with facility blocks, including Bonus Blocks and hittable blocks in Marks (but not Ice Blocks). Starting with v1.7.2, an MF-style Beetroot option was added - only MF-style Beetroots can interact with blocks.
- Starman Mario and Buzzy Beetle shells can now defeat Blue Flying Cheep Cheeps and Spiny Cheep Cheeps.
- In God Mode, pressing `0` ignores the current camera constraints. When the screen is locked, pressing `Backspace` releases the lock. Starting with v1.7.12, Camera Mode also displays Mario's current coordinates.
- Additional Settings gained Fast Retry and Faster Level Pass (the latter was added in v1.7.12). When enabled, Mario respawns much more quickly after dying without interrupting the background music, and the delay between reaching the goal and entering the next level is significantly reduced.
- Starting with v1.7.7, you can move objects by 16 pixels at a time. Hold the Accurate Move key and press `R`, `F`, `D`, or `G` (Up, Down, Left, and Right, respectively) to perform a single 16-pixel adjustment.
- Starting with v1.7.11, Switch Blocks, bonus items, scenery objects, and enemies support drag placement, eliminating the need to click repeatedly. The version also fixed a bug that could cause multiple coins or other items to be placed at the same position.
- Starting with v1.7.11, pressing `F1` during gameplay displays level information in the lower-left corner of the screen, while the window title shows the level author and the SMWP version used to create the level.
- v1.7.11 fixed a bug where the score awarded by the Goal rod did not match the displayed score graphic, and another bug that caused defeated Cheep Cheeps to appear 16 pixels too far to the left.
- Starting with v1.7.12, the first line of the level title is no longer forced to display the HUD's `WORLD` label. It can now contain any text or characters. In addition, Additional Settings includes an option to hide the HUD for the current level.
- Starting with v1.7.12, while placing Blocks, pressing the Sub Menu key (default: `Q`) toggles whether a matching solid is placed automatically at the same time.
- Starting with v1.7.12, the starting number of lives for playing `.smwl` levels can be customized.
